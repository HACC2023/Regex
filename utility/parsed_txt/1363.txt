Question: UH Alert Troubleshooting
Article Text: The UH Alert emergency text messaging system, http://www.hawaii.edu/alert , is used to send health and safety alerts to the UH community. The common causes for not receiving text messages from UH Alert include(#5 is most common): You are not registered to receive UH Alerts. UH Alert is an OPT-IN service, you must register for this service to receive alerts. Log into UH Alert and register your cell phone number. You have switched carriers or phone numbers and you forgot to update your UH Alert information. Log into UH Alert and update your cell phone information. You have mis-typed your cell phone number into the UH Alert system. Log into UH Alert and double-check your number. The number you registered in the UH Alert system is not a cell phone number. Many people input their office or home phone numbers. Log into UH Alert and double-check your number, make sure it's your cell phone number. Your number is not activated for text messaging for UH Alert or UH Alert is blacklisted by your provider Send a text message "START" (without quotes) to 84323 Wait for a return message saying service has been reactivated. If you get an error message contact your cell phone provider. If you get the message "Service access denied" see #6. Your cell phone provider is prohibiting third party text messages to your phone. Call your cell phone provider and ask if they are blocking 3rd party text messages, if they are, request to remove the block. Your cell phone is not capable of receiving text messages. Add text messaging service to your cell phone plan. There are various other errors that may prevent UH Alert from sending texts to your phone. Call your cell phone provider to troubleshoot texts coming from 84323. Your mobile phone service/carrier is not supported.
